Brown Bear

About the brown bear

In the heart of Alaska's wilderness, a massive brown bear named Koda roamed the banks of the salmon-rich river.
For years, Koda protected the river and its inhabitants from harm, earning him the title of Guardian of the River. One summer, a severe drought hit the land, and the river's waters began to dwindle.

The salmon struggled to survive, and the other bears grew hungry and aggressive. Koda knew he had to act. With his powerful sense of smell,
Koda sniffed out a hidden underground spring. He dug and clawed, creating a new channel to divert the spring's water into the river.
Slowly but surely, the river's waters began to rise, and the salmon population began to thrive once more.

Species

    Here are the eight species of brown bears:

  • 1. _Grizzly bear_ (Ursus arctos horribilis)
  • 2. _Kodiak bear_ (Ursus arctos middendorffi)
  • 3. _European brown bear_ (Ursus arctos arctos)
  • 4. _Siberian brown bear_ (Ursus arctos yesoensis)
  • 5. _Ussuri brown bear_ (Ursus arctos ussuricus)
  • 6. _Kamchatka brown bear_ (Ursus arctos beringianus)
  • 7. _Tibetan blue bear_ (Ursus arctos pruinosus)
  • 8. _Himalayan brown bear_ (Ursus arctos isabellinus)

    • Note: Some taxonomists recognize only one species of brown bear (Ursus arctos) with several subspecies, while others recognize multiple species.

Features

Koda's selfless act earned him respect and admiration. From that day forward, Koda watched over the river, ensuring the balance of nature remained intact.
